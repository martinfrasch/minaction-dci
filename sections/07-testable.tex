\section{Testable commitments}
\label{sec:testable}

The frame is falsifiable, and each prediction already has a first data point in the four-domain program. It predicts that (i)~systems combining a compressed vertical prior with an executable verifier show markedly higher skill-acquisition efficiency --- lower $E$ for equal $GD$ --- than scale-matched general agents on discovery tasks (the ${\sim}40\%$ training-energy reduction in physics law recovery \citep{frasch2026b}; and the energy-first evaluation across 2{,}203 NAS experiments \citep{frasch2026c} are the first measurements); (ii)~discovery quality tracks the \emph{compression} of the injected prior, not model size; (iii)~$\Prin$-guided search transfers across domains that share the invariant while general search does not --- the cross-modality NAS results \citep{frasch2026c} and the biology-scale modularity-excess replication \citep{frasch2026d} being the first cross-domain data points; (iv)~independent verifier-guided discovery systems built with no reference to this program --- FunSearch \citep{romera2024} and AlphaGeometry \citep{trinh2024} --- should admit a \emph{post hoc} mapping onto the action-principle formalism, with their evaluator/deduction engine playing the role of $\Rule$ and their generator's implicit objective falling in class$(\Prin)$; a system that provably resists such a mapping would count against the claim that \NWAP\ captures what makes these systems work. This last check is owed precisely because the evidence marshalled above is, so far, a single research program (\citealp{frasch2026a,frasch2026b,frasch2026c,frasch2026d}); back-testing the formalism on third-party architectures is the way that ecosystem is opened. Finally, (v)~discovery efficiency should fall monotonically with verifier fidelity $\rho$ and vanish below a domain-dependent floor $\rho^\ast$ (\S\ref{sec:scope}), with a more compressed prior raising tolerance to a weaker verifier. Each is a concrete experiment, not a slogan.\footnote{A companion benchmark suite operationalizing these predictions --- families~A (dynamics / law recovery) and~B (physiology / causal discovery), with executable generators, verifier interfaces, JSON schemas, and a sample suite --- is openly available at \url{https://github.com/martinfrasch/minaction-dci}.}
